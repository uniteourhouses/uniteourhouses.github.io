Concerning Hobbits

As you prepare for a night of feasting, drinking, dancing, laughing, and the loving companionship of friends and family, we encourage you to think about your evening attire!

Dress Attire: Hobbit Formal

Guests are encouraged to wear attire that fits a modern formal or black-tie optional dress code. Men should wear a suit with a shirt and tie or alternatively a suit jacket. Women should wear a floor-length gown, knee-length cocktail dress, a pantsuit, dressy separates, or other culturally appropriate attire. Alternatively, guests may wear medieval or renaissance period appropriate attire suitable for a prominent social gathering.

This page contains resources for any guests looking for additional information on procuring medieval or renaissance period attire.

Outfit Changes

Some guests have expressed a desire to bring two outfits, a modern formal wear and a historical outfit, with the intention of changing during or after the cocktail hour. The venue has large bathrooms that can accomondate outfit changes, so you are welcome to do whatever makes you most comfortable (committing to just a modern or historical outfit or changing through the evening).

Outfit Ideas

If you are looking to procure an outfit for the evening you may consider the following resources. Most importantly have fun!

Ben's engagement outfit was purchased from armstreet.com (with a few adjustments from Amber).

grimfrost.com has Viking outfits and accessories for men and women.

holyclothing.com has outfits for women.

Homemade

Etsy has many reputable vendors that sell a variety of unique outfits for both men and women. For those wishing to make their own outfit, cotton or linen fabric can be found in local fabric shops (e.g Joann fabric) or online (e.g. fabrics-store.com and fabricmartfabrics.com). There are many patterns for women's dresses avaiable (e.g. pattern 1, pattern 2, pattern 3, pattern 4).
